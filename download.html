Install

Load the beta in Chrome.

  1. Download and unzip the PRISM beta package.
  2. Open chrome://extensions and enable Developer Mode.
  3. Click Load unpacked and select the extension folder.
  4. Open an article and run a PRISM Quick Scan.

Current beta

What the beta does today.

The beta focuses on the browser extension experience: quick scans, clear highlights, and concise explanations that help readers decide what deserves a closer look.

Inline highlights

Reliability signals appear directly in the article text so users can review the page without switching tabs.

Hover explanations

Highlighted passages include short, plain-language explanations of the signal being surfaced.

Quick score

A page-level scan score summarizes the overall signal mix without claiming to prove what is true.

Deepfake detection roadmap

PRISM is being designed for future image and video provenance checks that help flag synthetic-media risk.

Article-first workflow

PRISM is browser-first and built around reading in place, not sending users into a separate research dashboard.

Roadmap-ready architecture

The beta lays the foundation for image provenance, saved reports, source-backed verification, and team workflows.
