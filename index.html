The four pillars

Every PRISM report is built from four signal pillars.

PRISM separates a noisy page into the core perspectives that shape a useful reliability review.

01 Language and framing

Looks for loaded wording, emotional framing, vague certainty, and language that may push readers before evidence is clear.

02 Source transparency

Checks whether claims are tied to named sources, primary documents, clear citations, or vague phrases like "experts say."

03 Claim support

Flags statistics, causal claims, and high-impact assertions that need stronger evidence or a clearer trail back to the source.

04 AI and media signals

Reviews AI-like writing patterns now and sets the foundation for image provenance, deepfake context, and synthetic-media review.
